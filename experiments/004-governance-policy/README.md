# Experiment 004: Socio-Economic Redesign - Governance & Policy
## Modeling UBI, 4-Day Work Weeks, and Systemic Policy Change

### Overview
Build an AI-powered policy simulator that models the real-world impacts of universal basic income (UBI), four-day work weeks, and other governance innovations across multiple countries and contexts. Goal: empower policymakers and researchers to test bold ideas before implementation.

### Inputs
- **Data**: PolicyEngine, Wharton PWBM,UN development databases, labor statistics, tax-benefit systems
- **Tools**: PolicyEngine API, microsimulation frameworks (microsim libraries), econometric models
- **Partners**: Economic Security Project, UBI Center, government economists, labor policy orgs
- **Scenarios**: UBI amounts ($500-1500/mo), phaseouts, work week reductions (4 vs 5 days)

### Outputs (6-month)
1. **Interactive Notebook**: "Simulating UBI & 4-Day Work Weeks Globally"
2. **Policy Simulator Dashboard**: Adjust parameters â†’ see impacts on poverty, inequality, labor supply, GDP
3. **3 Case Studies**: UBI ($500, $1000, $1500/mo) + 4-day week combinations, country-specific models
4. **Technical Brief**: "Human-Centered Policy Innovation via AI-Human Loops"
5. **Country Pilots**: Collaborate with 2+ governments on simulation-informed policy design
6. **Publication**: Policy white paper + peer-reviewed article
